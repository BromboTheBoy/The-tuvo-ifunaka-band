Ungazi — The Village

Tuvo Ifunaka Band formed in the village of Ungazi, where music has always been a communal act — sung at harvests, at crossroads, at the edge of rivers at dusk. The name Tuvo Ifunaka means something close to "voices that find each other" in the local tradition, and the group live up to every syllable of it.

Jaja, Petr, Tabbow, and Tsolita grew up in the same few square miles of Ungazi, each shaped by different corners of its musical life. They began playing together informally, then couldn't stop. What started as late evenings on somebody's porch has grown into something that has drawn crowds across the region.

Their debut album, Ungazi The Village, is their way of fixing that place and its stories in sound — before time moves on and changes it. It is an act of preservation as much as it is an act of art.
